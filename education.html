❤️ Why Donate Blood?

🏥

Save Lives

One donation can save up to 3 lives. Accident victims, surgery patients and cancer patients all need blood.

💪

Health Benefits

Regular donation reduces risk of heart disease, lowers iron levels and stimulates new blood cell production.

🧪

Free Health Check

Before donation you get a mini health screening — blood pressure, pulse, haemoglobin and more checked free!

🌍

No Substitute

Blood cannot be manufactured. The only source is human donors. Your donation is irreplaceable.

⏱️

Only 30 Minutes

The actual blood donation takes only 8–10 minutes. The whole process including registration is under 30 mins.

🎖️

Be a Hero

Blood donors are real-life heroes. You may never meet the person you save, but your impact lasts forever.

✅ Who Can Donate?

✅

Age 18–65 years

You must be between 18 and 65 years old to donate blood.

✅

Weight above 45 kg

Minimum body weight of 45 kg is required for safe donation.

✅

Haemoglobin ≥ 12.5 g/dL

Adequate haemoglobin level ensures safe donation for both donor and recipient.

✅

90 days since last donation

You must wait at least 90 days (3 months) between whole blood donations.

❌

Fever or cold recently

Wait at least 2 weeks after recovering from fever, cold or flu.

❌

Pregnant or breastfeeding

Women who are pregnant or breastfeeding should not donate blood.

❌

On certain medications

Antibiotics, blood thinners and some other medications may disqualify you temporarily.

❌

Recent tattoo or piercing

Wait 6 months after getting a tattoo or body piercing before donating.

🔍 Myths vs Facts

Donating blood is painful and dangerous
Blood donation is safe and involves only a small needle prick. Donors feel minimal discomfort and the body replenishes blood within 24–48 hours.
You can get infected by donating blood
All equipment used is sterile and disposable. There is absolutely zero risk of infection from donating blood at a certified blood bank.
Donating blood makes you weak for weeks
Your body replaces the fluid within 24 hours and red blood cells within 4–6 weeks. Most donors feel completely normal within a few hours.
People with diabetes or high BP can never donate
People with well-controlled diabetes or blood pressure are often eligible to donate. Always consult the blood bank staff who will check your vitals.

🏥 What Happens When You Donate?

1

Registration

Fill in a short form with your personal and health details. Takes about 5 minutes.

2

Health Screening

A quick check of your blood pressure, pulse, temperature and haemoglobin level.

3

Blood Donation

Around 350–450 ml of blood is collected. The actual process takes only 8–10 minutes.

4

Rest & Refreshments

You rest for 10–15 minutes and are given juice and snacks before leaving.

5

You're a Hero! 🎉

Your blood is tested, processed and sent to hospitals where it saves up to 3 lives.

💡 Did You Know?

1

Every 2 seconds, someone in India needs blood. That's over 40,000 units needed every single day.

2

Blood cannot be manufactured in a lab. The only source of blood is voluntary human donors.

3

Only 7% of people have O- blood, making it the most critical blood type in emergencies.

4

One whole blood donation can be separated into red cells, plasma and platelets — helping up to 3 different patients.

5

India needs about 12 million units of blood annually but only collects about 9 million — a 25% shortage.
